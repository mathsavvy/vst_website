History Of Vedic Time Format

The first ever time-format was mentioned in Ancient Indian scripture : Rig Veda. The ancient Indians named it 'Muhurut'. This time-format is calculated from one sunrise to another. Between two sunrises, there are 30 Hours(Muhurut), 30 Minutes(Kaal) and 30 seconds(Kastha), which means ancient Indians calculated and kept track of a 30 hour time-format. As the technology was mechnical till 20th century, the clocks based on this advanced time system couldn't be made.

As India fought many battles in last 1000 years till 1947 and much of our knowledge along with the sciptures was demolished. In last 600 years, the 24 hr time format became trend as whole world accepted it and gave no thought to 'Alternate Time-formats'. Since technology has enhanced to Digital, we can now build clocks on Our Ancient Indian Time-format which starts from sunrise.

Difference between Current Time format and Vedic Standard Time

Current time-format makes 24 equal devision of the day and is a Mechnical Time-format that works the same every day. That's why sunrise varies everyday in the Current Time System.

Vedic Time format is a Digital Time-format as it starts from sunrise (in Vedic time system, date changes from sunrise). At 00:00 hrs it, is always sunrise. This is the advancement in Vedic Time System.

As our current time format has divided world in 24 time zones, many countries do not get proper daylight, thus daylight savings time. In Alternate time-format(Vedic Time System), there can again be 30 time zones based on sunrise. Even every country can have their specific time-zone in future and the bigger countries which have big differences between longitudes at their ends, can use multiple Vedic time-zones based on variation of sunrise across the country.
